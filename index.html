What is the Settlement?

In 2005, U.S. Merchants brought a class-action lawsuit against VISA, Mastercard and their bank affiliates for allegedly inflating the interchange fees they charged Merchants for credit card processing. The lawsuit was settled in 2018, but the settlement amount and processes for its distribution were not determined until late 2023. Any Merchant or Business who accepted VISA and/or Mastercard from January 1, 2004 to January 25, 2019 is likely eligible to receive a portion of the settlement amount.

FAQs

What was the class action lawsuit about?

This class action lawsuit addresses the interchange fees charged to merchants who accepted Visa or Mastercard credit or debit cards between January 1, 2004, and January 25, 2019, and the rules enforced by Visa and Mastercard. The plaintiffs allege that Visa, Mastercard, and their member banks unlawfully set these fees and imposed rules that prevented merchants from steering customers to other payment methods, keeping the fees high.

What is an interchange fee?

When a cardholder uses a credit or debit card for a purchase, an interchange fee of about 1% to 2% is incurred. These fees constitute the largest portion of the costs merchants pay for accepting Visa and Mastercard, which update their rates biannually and publish them online.
